Portable Venipuncture Robot: Autonomous Vascular Access

Haoyang Li1*, Tenghui Xie1*, Jinghai Pan1, Yibo Zhao1, Zhifan Yan1, Jinyan Guo1,3, Yu Chen1, Junbo Ge2, Peng Qi1
1Tongji University, 2Fudan University, 3Shanghai Tenth People's Hospital
Paper Code Data

Our Venipuncture Robot performs autonomous vascular access with high precision and safety.

Abstract

We present a portable robotic device capable of performing autonomous vascular access with minimal supervision. Our 9-DOF robot combines NIR imaging and ultrasound technology to accurately locate and access veins, addressing challenges in traditional venipuncture procedures.

The robot features a compact design with positioning, support, information processing, and puncturing units. It utilizes advanced algorithms for vein segmentation and real-time needle guidance, ensuring high precision and safety during the procedure.

Our experimental results demonstrate a success rate of 98.24% in simulated tests, with a maximum puncture error of 1.79mm. This innovation has the potential to significantly improve patient care, reduce the workload on medical staff, and minimize the risk of complications in venipuncture procedures.

Video

Robot Design

Our venipuncture robot features a 9-DOF design with multiple units for precise vascular access. The compact structure includes positioning, support, information processing, and puncturing units, along with an advanced needle module.

Vein Detection

The robot employs a dual imaging system combining NIR and ultrasound technologies for accurate vein detection and depth estimation. Our advanced algorithms process these images to determine the optimal puncture site and angle.

Experimental Results

Our experiments demonstrate the high accuracy and reliability of the venipuncture robot. With a success rate of 98.24% in simulated tests and a maximum puncture error of 1.79mm, the robot shows promising results for clinical applications.
